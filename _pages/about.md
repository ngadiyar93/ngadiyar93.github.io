I am an assistant professor at the University of Notre Dame, where I direct the [AMPERE Lab](https://ampere-lab.github.io). 

Prior to joining Notre Dame, I was a member of the R&D staff at the [Oak Ridge National Laboratory (ORNL)](https://www.ornl.gov/staff-profile/fnu-nishanth), where I also held the Alvin M. Weinberg [distinguished staff fellowship](https://www.ornl.gov/careers/distinguished-fellowships).

My research focuses on developing **high-performance and sustainable electro-mechanical systems**. I work at the intersection of *electric machines, power-electronics,* and *physics-based control*
to create devices and systems that can efficiently and sustainably convert energy between electrical (e.g., currents, voltages) and mechanical forms (i.e., position, speed, torque, force). Learn more about my research [here.](https://nishanth.work/research/)

I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Wisconsin-Madison, where I worked with [Eric Severson](https://elev.umn.edu/) at the [Wisconsin Electric Machines and Power Electronics Consortium (WEMPEC)](https://wempec.wisc.edu/). 
I have also worked in the industry, first with the Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories (MERL), and most recently with the General Electric (GE) Research Center (now GE Aerospace Research).
